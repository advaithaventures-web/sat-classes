Why Reading & Writing?

While I genuinely love mathematics and would enjoy teaching Digital SAT Math, I've realized something important: there are already many excellent math coaches doing outstanding work. Students have quality options for quantitative preparation.

Reading & Writing is different. Here, students—especially those with strong analytical minds who excel at math—struggle with what they perceive as subjectivity. They want objective, logical answers. They want problems they can solve with certainty.

Here's what I tell them: Digital SAT Reading & Writing is completely objective. Every single answer is demonstrably, logically correct. There is no subjectivity, no "best guess," no "it depends on interpretation." The questions are constructed with the same precision as mathematical problems. You simply need to learn the framework for seeing it.

Students who are naturally good at math approach Reading & Writing expecting the same kind of clear, logical reasoning—and they're right to expect it. It's there. But without specialized guidance, they can't see the patterns. They mistake unfamiliarity for ambiguity.

This is where I focus my expertise: showing analytically-minded students that Reading & Writing questions are just as objective as math questions—they simply require a different analytical framework. Once you have that framework, every answer becomes clear, logical, and provable.

Teaching Philosophy

What I Believe

  • Reading & Writing is objective, not subjective. Every answer can be proven correct through systematic analysis. My job is teaching you that analytical framework.
  • Small groups are non-negotiable. With more than four students, personalized attention becomes impossible. Every student has unique weaknesses that deserve individual focus.
  • Quality over everything. I use only official College Board materials and expertly crafted questions. Students deserve the real thing, not approximations.
  • Mastery takes time and patience. There are no shortcuts to a 750+ score. What there is: systematic instruction, cumulative learning, and genuine expertise.
  • This is not mass education. I work with students who are already good and want to become exceptional. Students who understand that excellence requires commitment.
  • Results speak louder than marketing. Every student I've taught came through word-of-mouth referrals. That's the only metric that matters.
